# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Ouja Residence operators, managers, and the owner use this dashboard while running daily short-term-rental operations in Riyadh. They work across guest replies, escalations, arrivals, cleaning, maintenance, pricing, revenue, listings, design requests, and PMO tasks. The main context is time pressure: they need to know what needs attention now, what can wait, and which action is safe to take.

## Product Purpose

The dashboard is the Arabic-first control center for Ouja Residence operations. It turns Hostaway, Discord, Airbnb, pricing, and internal workflow data into one place for scanning risk, approving guest communication, tracking readiness, reviewing revenue, and applying operational decisions. Success means an operator can open the dashboard and confidently answer: what is urgent, who owns it, what action is next, and what will happen if I click.

## Brand Personality

Calm, sharp, and practical. The tone should feel local to a Saudi operations team: direct Arabic, natural English when needed, and no corporate fluff. The interface should feel trusted and focused, with warmth from the Ouja identity but no decorative noise.

## Anti-references

Do not make it look like a marketing landing page, a generic SaaS template, a pastel analytics demo, or a decorative card grid. Avoid oversized hero sections, ornamental gradients, glass effects, vague AI-style copy, and color that does not carry operational meaning. Do not hide risky actions behind cute labels or unclear icons.

## Design Principles

1. Urgency must be visible before volume.
2. Every risky action must explain its consequence before it runs.
3. Arabic, English, unit codes, money, and dates must remain readable together.
4. Empty states should teach the next action or explain missing data.
5. Dense pages are allowed, but the hierarchy must make the first decision obvious.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default target is WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-visible focus, clear button labels, reduced-motion support, and reliable touch targets. The product is Arabic-first with English support, so RTL/LTR mixing must be handled deliberately. Functional states should not rely on color alone; counts, labels, icons, and copy should reinforce severity.
