# DYAR 20 Owner Performance Report — Design Specification

**Date:** 2026-07-13  
**Status:** Approved for execution  
**Audience:** Owner of DYAR 20, Al Qirawan, Riyadh  
**Reporting period:** H1 2026, 1 January through 30 June

## Communication job

By the end of the presentation, the owner should understand how DYAR 20 performed in H1 2026, how that performance compares with the Riyadh short-term rental market and nearby two-bedroom competitors, what produced the result, and what H2 2026 could deliver under three transparent scenarios.

## Approved decisions

- Cover title: **DYAR 20 Performance Report | تقرير أداء ديار 20**.
- One bilingual deck. Arabic leads; concise English follows within the same slide.
- Do not discuss operating expenses or include an expense section.
- Use SAR throughout.
- Primary period: H1 2026. December 2025 may appear only as launch context if needed.
- Compare against two layers: Riyadh short-term-rental market data and a public competitor set of similar two-bedroom listings in Al Qirawan.
- Use the reservation export dated 13 July 2026 as the source of actual performance.

## Analytical definitions

- **Accommodation revenue:** Airbnb listing base price plus direct-booking total value. This avoids treating Airbnb guest service charges as property revenue.
- **Expected payout:** Airbnb expected payout plus direct-booking total value.
- **Occupancy:** unique occupied calendar nights divided by 181 available nights. Three overlapping reservation dates on 24–26 June count once for occupancy.
- **ADR:** accommodation revenue divided by booked reservation nights.
- **RevPAR:** accommodation revenue divided by all available nights.
- Cross-month reservations are allocated evenly by booked night.
- Public competitor data describes listing position, rating, review depth, capacity, and highlighted amenities. It does not claim competitor occupancy or revenue.
- Riyadh market figures are directional because the citywide dataset includes multiple property types and neighborhoods.

## Core findings to communicate

- H1 accommodation revenue: **SAR 98.4k**.
- Expected payout: **SAR 81.8k**.
- Occupancy: **86.2%**, based on 156 unique occupied nights.
- ADR: **SAR 619**; RevPAR: **SAR 544**.
- 63 reservations overlapped the period. Airbnb contributed 60 reservations and 94.6% of accommodation revenue.
- Median booking lead time was one day; 74.6% of reservations arrived within three days of booking.
- One-night stays represented 54.0% of reservations.
- Friday and Saturday were strongest; Sunday and Monday had the most visible occupancy headroom.
- Public listing snapshots show strong guest satisfaction for DYAR 20, but a smaller review base than established Al Qirawan competitors.

## Narrative and slide architecture

1. Minimal cover with the DYAR 20 exterior image.
2. Executive answer: strong first-half demand with concentrated channel and booking-window exposure.
3. H1 scorecard with the five owner-level KPIs.
4. Monthly trajectory showing revenue, occupancy, and ADR.
5. Revenue quality showing Airbnb concentration and expected payout.
6. Booking behavior showing short lead times.
7. Stay pattern and weekday performance showing one-night dependence and Sunday/Monday opportunity.
8. Riyadh market comparison using AirDNA figures converted at SAR 3.75 per USD.
9. Al Qirawan competitor comparison using four public two-bedroom listings.
10. Performance drivers and constraints, connected directly to evidence.
11. H2 2026 scenarios: conservative, base, and upside.
12. A focused 90-day plan for rate protection, weekday demand, longer stays, direct share, and review depth.
13. Owner dashboard for the next quarterly review with explicit thresholds.
14. Methodology and sources.

## Visual system

- 16:9, 1280 × 720.
- Warm ivory canvas, deep forest typography, muted gold accent, rust only for warnings.
- Arabic-first hierarchy using IBM Plex Sans Arabic where available; Arial fallback for reliable rendering. English uses Aptos/Arial.
- Deck title at least 50 pt, slide titles at least 35 pt, body copy at least 16 pt.
- Flat editorial compositions with generous alignment, thin rules, and native charts. Avoid nested cards, glossy gradients, and decorative dashboards.
- Use the existing DYAR 20 complex photograph once on the cover. Charts and tables carry the remaining visual evidence.
- Sources appear in small footers on research slides and in full on the final methodology slide.

## Projection method

H2 scenarios use the apartment's H1 occupancy and ADR as the base:

- Conservative: occupancy 10 points below H1 and ADR 5% below H1.
- Base: H1 occupancy and ADR continue across 184 H2 nights.
- Upside: occupancy rises three points, capped below 92%, and ADR rises 7%.

The scenarios are planning ranges, not guaranteed outcomes. The deck shows each assumption beside its projected accommodation revenue.
